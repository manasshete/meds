Paracetamol

Also known as: Acetaminophen, Crocin, Dolo, Calpol

Pain Relief Fever Reduction OTC

Paracetamol is a commonly used medication for treating pain and fever. It's available over-the-counter and is considered one of the safest pain medications when used correctly.

Overview

Drug Class:
Analgesic (pain reliever) and antipyretic (fever reducer)
Available as:
Tablets, capsules, liquid suspensions, suppositories, intravenous solutions
Prescription:
Available over-the-counter (OTC) in most countries
Common Brands in India:
Crocin, Dolo, Calpol, Paracip, Febridol

Paracetamol (also known as acetaminophen) is one of the most widely used medications globally for pain relief and fever reduction. Unlike NSAIDs (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs), paracetamol has minimal anti-inflammatory effects but is generally well-tolerated and has fewer gastrointestinal side effects.

The exact mechanism of action is not fully understood, but it is believed to work by inhibiting the synthesis of prostaglandins in the central nervous system and peripherally blocking pain impulse generation. It may also affect the body's heat regulating center (hypothalamus) to reduce fever.

Key Points

  • Effective for mild to moderate pain and fever
  • Generally well-tolerated when used as directed
  • Does not cause stomach irritation like NSAIDs
  • Safe for most people including pregnant women (under medical supervision)
  • Overdose can cause severe liver damage

Uses

Paracetamol is used to treat a variety of conditions involving pain and fever. It's particularly useful when anti-inflammatory medications like ibuprofen or aspirin are unsuitable.

Headaches

Effective for tension headaches and can help with migraines when combined with other medications.

Fever

Reduces body temperature in febrile conditions like flu, common cold, and other infections.

Dental Pain

Relieves toothaches and pain after dental procedures.

Muscle Pain

Helps with muscle aches, sprains, and strains.

Childhood Fever

Often preferred for children's fever as it has fewer side effects than aspirin.

Post-operative Pain

Used as part of pain management after surgery, often in combination with other analgesics.

Note: Paracetamol is not effective for all types of pain. It has minimal effect on inflammation and may not be suitable for conditions where inflammation is the primary cause of pain, such as rheumatoid arthritis.

Dosage

The correct dosage of paracetamol depends on age, weight, and the specific formulation being used. It's crucial not to exceed the recommended daily dose as overdose can cause serious liver damage.

Standard Dosage Guidelines

Age Group Single Dose Frequency Maximum Daily Dose
Adults and children over 12 years 500-1000 mg Every 4-6 hours as needed 4000 mg (4 g)
Children 6-12 years 250-500 mg Every 4-6 hours as needed 2000 mg (2 g)
Children 2-5 years 120-250 mg Every 4-6 hours as needed 1000 mg (1 g)
Children 3-11 months 60-120 mg Every 4-6 hours as needed 480 mg

Important Dosage Warnings

  • Do not exceed the stated dose
  • Allow at least 4 hours between doses
  • For children, dosing by weight is more accurate (10-15 mg/kg per dose)
  • Reduced dosage may be necessary for elderly patients or those with liver/kidney problems
  • If you're taking other medications containing paracetamol, include these in your daily total

Special Populations

Elderly: May require dose reduction due to decreased liver function.

Liver Impairment: Lower doses or extended intervals between doses may be necessary.

Kidney Impairment: Severe kidney disease may require extended intervals between doses.

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding: Generally considered safe but should be used under medical supervision.

Side Effects

When taken at recommended doses, paracetamol has relatively few side effects compared to other pain relievers. However, all medications can cause unwanted effects in some people.

Common Side Effects (rare at normal doses)

  • Nausea
  • Rash
  • Headache (paradoxical)

Rare Side Effects

  • Blood disorders (thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, neutropenia)
  • Allergic reactions (skin rash, itching)
  • Hypotension (when given intravenously)

Serious Side Effects (seek immediate medical attention)

  • Severe skin reactions (Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis)
  • Signs of liver damage (yellowing of skin/eyes, dark urine, abdominal pain, unusual tiredness)
  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)

Overdose Warning

Paracetamol overdose is a medical emergency that can cause irreversible liver damage and death. Early symptoms may be mild or absent but can progress to liver failure within days.

Symptoms of overdose include:

  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Abdominal pain
  • Confusion, drowsiness
  • Yellowing of the skin and whites of the eyes (jaundice)

If overdose is suspected, seek emergency medical help immediately, even if there are no symptoms.

Precautions

While paracetamol is generally safe for most people, certain precautions should be taken to ensure its safe use.

Liver Disease

Use with caution if you have liver problems. Lower doses may be necessary.

Alcohol Use

Regular alcohol consumption increases the risk of liver damage from paracetamol.

Kidney Disease

Severe kidney disease may require dose adjustment.

Allergies

Avoid if you've had allergic reactions to paracetamol previously.

Pregnancy

Generally considered safe but use only as directed by healthcare provider.

Other Medications

Check all medications for paracetamol content to avoid accidental overdose.

Duration of Use

Do not take paracetamol for more than 3 days for fever or 5 days for pain without consulting a healthcare professional. Prolonged use without medical supervision may mask underlying conditions requiring different treatment.

Interactions

Paracetamol can interact with certain medications, affecting either its effectiveness or increasing the risk of side effects.

Medication/Substance Interaction Recommendation
Warfarin and other anticoagulants May enhance anticoagulant effect with prolonged use Monitor INR closely if using regularly
Alcohol Increases risk of liver damage Limit alcohol consumption
Carbamazepine, phenytoin, phenobarbital May reduce paracetamol effectiveness and increase liver toxicity risk Dose adjustment may be necessary
Isoniazid May increase risk of liver toxicity Monitor liver function
Chloramphenicol Paracetamol may increase chloramphenicol concentrations Monitor for increased side effects
Zidovudine (AZT) May increase risk of neutropenia Monitor blood counts
Probenecid May reduce paracetamol elimination Lower paracetamol doses may be needed
Cholestyramine Reduces paracetamol absorption Take paracetamol 1 hour before or 4-6 hours after cholestyramine

Note: Always inform your healthcare provider about all medications, supplements, and herbal products you are taking before starting paracetamol.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is paracetamol the same as acetaminophen?

Yes, paracetamol and acetaminophen are two different names for exactly the same drug. Paracetamol is the name commonly used in India, the UK, Australia, and many other countries, while acetaminophen is the term used primarily in the United States.

Can I take paracetamol and ibuprofen together?

Yes, paracetamol and ibuprofen can be taken together as they work in different ways. This combination is often more effective for pain relief than either medication alone. You can take them at the same time or stagger them throughout the day. However, always follow the recommended dosage for each medication and consult a healthcare professional if you're unsure.

Is paracetamol safe during pregnancy?

Paracetamol is generally considered the safest pain reliever for use during pregnancy when used as directed. However, it should only be taken when necessary and at the lowest effective dose for the shortest possible time. Always consult with your healthcare provider before taking any medication during pregnancy.

How long does it take for paracetamol to work?

Paracetamol typically begins to work within 30-60 minutes after taking it. The pain-relieving effects usually last for 4-6 hours. If you have taken the recommended dose and don't feel relief after an hour, do not take more without consulting a healthcare professional.

Can children take paracetamol?

Yes, paracetamol is approved for use in children and is available in various child-specific formulations like syrups and drops. However, the dosage must be carefully calculated based on the child's weight and age. Always use the measuring device provided with the medication and follow the package instructions or your doctor's advice.

What should I do if I miss a dose?

Paracetamol is usually taken as needed rather than on a fixed schedule. If you're taking it regularly and miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember. However, if it's almost time for your next dose, skip the missed dose and continue with your regular schedule. Do not take a double dose to make up for a missed one.
